Isaac Mayala's Rugby Journey

My rugby journey began at the age of 12 when I joined the U13 team. Before rugby, I was primarily focused on cricket, soccer, and track & field. My first rugby coach, Bevan Hope (known as Mr. Hope), introduced me to the sport after noticing my skills on the soccer field, cricket pitch, and my speed on the track. In soccer, I started as a striker before transitioning to left back and center back positions. In cricket, I excelled as both a batsman and wicket keeper, while in track & field, I competed as a sprinter and in the decathlon.

Throughout my career, I've drawn inspiration from remarkable athletes like Shaun Johnson, Rieko Iaone, Cheslin Kolbe, JJ Taulagi, Seabelo Senatla, Joe Mano, and my mentor Ina Futi who plays for the Seattle Seawolves. These role models have fueled my ambitions to compete in the Olympics, play for international clubs, represent the United States national team, and participate in both the 7s World Cup and 15s competitions.

I pride myself on being hardworking, coachable, and eager to learn anything that will positively impact my future. My values center around being helpful, family-oriented, trustworthy, and maintaining a strong connection to God throughout my athletic journey.
